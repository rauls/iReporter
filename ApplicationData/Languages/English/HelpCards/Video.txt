<!--OVERVIEW-->
This report highlights the most popular video clips being accessed on your site. This includes both streamed files and downloaded video files. Use this information to assess your bandwidth requirements - video clips are generally quite large in size, and will apply greater pressure on your Internet connection than normal web pages.

Note that for a file to be recognized as an video clip, its extension (such as .asf) must be specified in <i>Settings > Analysis > Extensions > Video</i>.

<!--GRAPH-->
<b>Vertical axis:</b> Video Content.
URL of the video clip being analyzed.

<b>Horizontal axis:</b>  Hits (default sorting). 
Hits to the corresponding video clip. Changing the sorting options in <i>Settings > Statistics</i> will alter the horizontal axis to the new sort method. This report can be sorted/graphed by hits, bytes or errors.

<b>Red lines (if present):</b>
HTTP errors (Page not found, server error, etc)

<!--TABLE-->
<b>Files:</b>
URL of the video clip being analyzed.

<b>Hits (%):</b>
Number of hits to the corresponding video clip. (Percentage as a proportion of hits to all video clips.)

<b>Bytes (%):</b>
Bytes transferred as a result of hits to the corresponding video clip. (Percentage as a proportion of bytes transferred for all video clips.)

<b>Errors:</b>
Errors generated as a result of hits to the corresponding video clip.
